Detailed Description

Perfect-gas thermodynamic model with constant \(C_p\).

constantCp implements a thermodynamic model in which the specific heat at constant pressure is assumed to be constant.

The model reads:

  • \(C_p\) from the thermodynamics sub-dictionary
  • Molecular weight from the specie sub-dictionary

The following thermodynamic properties are then derived:

  • Gas constant: \(R = \frac{R_u}{W}\)
  • Specific heat at constant volume: \(C_v = C_p - R\)
  • Heat-capacity ratio: \(\gamma = \frac{C_p}{C_v}\)

Energy relations:

  • Internal energy: \(e(T) = C_v T\)
  • Enthalpy: \(h(T) = C_p T\)

Speed of sound:

\[ a = \sqrt{\gamma R T} \]

Applicability
  • Ideal-gas equation of state
  • Perfect-gas thermodynamics
  • Compressible DG solvers
Limitations
  • Does not include formation enthalpy
  • Not suitable for real-gas or reacting-flow thermodynamics
