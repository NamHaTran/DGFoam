Detailed Description

Base class for thermodynamic laws in DG solvers.

thermoLaw defines the thermodynamic closure relations required by DG thermophysical models. It provides a unified interface for:

  • Converting between temperature and energy forms \((e, h)\)
  • Computing heat capacities \(C_p(T)\), \(C_v(T)\)
  • Computing heat-capacity ratio \(\gamma(T)\)
  • Computing speed of sound \(a(T)\)
  • Evaluating internal energy and enthalpy at Gauss points

All operations are performed locally at Gauss points of each DG cell, consistent with high-order discontinuous Galerkin formulations.

Thermodynamic model categories
Perfect-gas thermodynamics
  • \(C_p(T)\) and \(C_v(T)\) depend only on temperature
  • No formation enthalpy is included
  • \(h(T) = \int C_p(T)\,\mathrm{d}T\)
  • \(e(T) = \int C_v(T)\,\mathrm{d}T\)
  • Compatible with ideal-gas EOS: \(p = \rho R T\)
  • Used in compressible and high-temperature gas flows
Absolute-enthalpy thermodynamics
  • Enthalpy includes chemical formation energy
  • \(h(T) = h_f^0 + \int C_p(T)\,\mathrm{d}T\)
  • Required for combustion and reacting flows
  • Works with ideal-gas or selected real-gas EOS
Real-gas thermodynamics
  • \(C_p\), \(C_v\), \(e\), and \(h\) depend on both temperature and density
  • Must be used together with real-gas EOS
  • Required for high-pressure and supercritical fluids
Kinetic / rarefied-gas thermodynamics
  • Based on kinetic theory and molecular degrees of freedom
  • \(e = \frac{f}{2} R T\)
  • \(h = \frac{f+2}{2} R T\)
  • \(C_p\) and \(C_v\) follow kinetic DOF relations
  • Compatible with thermal-perfect-gas EOS
  • Used in rarefied gas and hypersonic applications
